# Audio Tour Script (3–5 minutes)

**Goal:** introduce yourself, explain how the portfolio is organized, highlight
your strongest work, reflect on your growth, and briefly note your design choices.

**Tips for recording**
- Aim for a calm, conversational pace (~150 words/minute → this script runs ~4 min).
- Record in a quiet room; a phone's voice-memo app is fine.
- Export as **MP3**, name it `tour.mp3`, and place it in `assets/audio/`.
- Read it once to warm up, then record. Small stumbles are human — keep going.

---

### Introduction (~30 sec)

> Hi, and welcome to my ePortfolio. My name is Brandon Davenport. I'm a
> commissioned officer in the United States Space Force, a graduate of
> organizational communication, and a software builder. For nearly a decade I've
> worked where precision matters — directing real-time space operations and
> leading teams under pressure — and along the way I discovered that my favorite
> problems live right where people and technology meet. This portfolio is my
> attempt to show you that story.

### How the portfolio is organized (~45 sec)

> The site is organized into a few clear sections you can reach from the tabs at
> the top. The **About** page tells my professional story in full. The **Résumé**
> page lays out my military experience, my four academic credentials, and my
> technical skills. The **Artifacts** section is the heart of the portfolio —
> three projects, each with an honest reflection on what I learned. And the
> **Goals** and **Contact** pages share where I'm headed and how to reach me. I
> kept the navigation identical on every page so it's always easy to find your way
> around.

### Highlighting my strongest work (~90 sec)

> If you only have time for one thing, visit the Artifacts section. My proudest
> project is **BARAD-DUR Tracker** — a full-stack, AI-augmented project-management
> platform I designed and built from an empty repository. It runs on a Python and
> FastAPI back end, pushes live updates over WebSockets, and includes a custom AI
> layer that understands natural-language requests. Building it taught me to think
> in systems and to integrate AI responsibly, not just as a gimmick.
>
> The second artifact is a **website I built for a nonprofit parent–teacher
> association**. That project was less about code and more about people — analyzing
> an audience of busy military and civilian families and making the organization's
> mission clear, welcoming, and accessible. It's where I really learned that design
> is communication.
>
> The third artifact is a **technical writing sample** from this course, which
> shows how I plan for a reader, structure information, and revise for clarity.

### Reflecting on my growth (~45 sec)

> Putting this portfolio together made my own growth obvious to me. A few years
> ago I was writing small automation scripts. Today I can build and ship a working
> application, design an accessible website, and explain technical work clearly to
> almost any audience. The thread connecting all of it is a commitment to
> clarity — making complex things understandable and genuinely useful.

### Design choices and closing (~30 sec)

> A quick note on the design: I chose a navy-and-gold palette with two simple
> fonts to keep things professional and easy to read, and I leaned on white space
> and consistent structure so the content — not the decoration — stays front and
> center. Thank you for taking the tour. I'd love to connect, so please reach out
> through the Contact page. Take a look around, and enjoy the portfolio.
